30% Of Bitcoin Supply Enters Into Loss As Price Continues Decline

RAKESH UPADHYAY

Posted on FEB 24, 2022 - 4 comments

On-chain data reveals more than 30% of the Bitcoin supply has entered into losses as the price of the crypto continues to decline. The metric works by looking at each coin on the chain and checking its history to see the price it last moved at. If this price is less than the current one, then the coin has accumulated some profit. On the other hand, it being bought at a higher price would imply that it’s in loss at the moment.

Less Than 70% Of Bitcoin Entities Are Now In Profit

As per the latest weekly report from Glassnode, the percentage of BTC entities in profit has severely dropped recently as the value now oscillates between 76% and 65%. The “percent of entities in profit” is an indicator that measures the percentage of Bitcoin investors that are currently in profit.

BTC Price

Bitcoin’s price crashed below the $37k mark early today, but has since recovered a bit. At the time of writing, the crypto’s price floats around $37.4k, down 15% in the last seven days. Over the past month, the coin has gained 3% in value.
below chart shows the trend in the price of BTC over the last five days.

Sustained Liquidations Deal Heavy Blow On Bitcoin Prices

However, Bitcoin’s long liquidations continued, with more than $30 million drained in just one hour, the second-highest hourly amount in four days and the third-highest hourly amount in nearly a month. The Bitcoin price drop appeared to shake up the market and market players; nevertheless, the coin seemed to make some attempts at recovery.
